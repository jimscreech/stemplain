What is STEMplain?

Have you ever had a super smart professor who just wasn’t able to break the topic down to a level easy enough for you to understand?

Or, hopped endlessly from site to site, the night before a test, hoping to find something or someone who’s already explained the topic you can’t wrap your head around?

STEMplain was made for you, with frustrations like this in mind. It is meant to be the smart kid who knew just how to explain a topic for the whole class to understand.

Why was STEMplain made?

I’m Kyle, STEMplain’s founder, and I’m a biochemistry major. I know firsthand how hard STEM classes can be.

As a STEM major, finding a good resource to explain difficult-to-understand topics in different subjects like Biology, Biochemistry, Organic chemistry etc., were always behind a paywall or didn’t clarify things well enough for me.

It’s intended to help you understand a hard topic by being engaging, easy to follow and most of all completely FREE.

I’m no leading expert, but from one student to another, I try my best to provide credible, and true information to help you understand.

Who is STEMplain for?

STEMplain is made for college students. The topics covered are college-level but is free to be used by anyone wanting to understand the sciences.

This is meant to help you understand a topic, not to teach you from scratch. Some prior understanding of the topic is best suited for the material, even if it’s just a little.

For those who wish to understand, and not just copy, this was made with you in mind.

How does it work?

LEARN: Find a topic you need to understand.

Browse the list of available topics we have and start learning fast. Learn an entire module or find a specific subtopic in a module.

TEACH: Study with Stanley

Research has found that having students teach new concepts to others enhances understanding and recall. Learn alongside Stanley, your study partner, and teach him what you learned.

HELP: Think you understand it, and can explain it better?

Give it a shot. This isn't a lame discussion board in your class. Feel free to post an explanation to the topic you needed help with once you understand it. Help someone else, others might rate it.

Don't have what you need? Suggest a topic to be explained.

Help out future you, and others.
